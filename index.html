      Optimal Conditions & Thermodynamic Theory

      Best Configuration: Thermophilic Temperature (55°C) • Thermoanaerobacter or Clostridium • Food Waste or Cheese Whey • Applied Voltage: 0.6V.

      Theoretical Basis: According to thermodynamic principles and the Van't Hoff equation, bio-hydrogen synthesis (e.g., converting glucose to acetate) is an endothermic reaction (ΔH° > 0). Elevating the temperature to 55°C increases the equilibrium constant, shifting the system to heavily favor $H_2$ production. Thermophilic conditions maximize the activity of the hydrogenase enzyme while naturally suppressing non-spore-forming, hydrogen-consuming microbes (like methanogens). Applying an optimal 0.6V external boost provides exactly enough thermodynamic "push" to drive cathodic reduction without relying heavily on grid electricity, keeping the system green.
